# Technical Analysis Reference

## Market Structure First

Before any indicator, read the market structure:
- **Higher highs + higher lows (HH/HL)** = uptrend. Bias: long
- **Lower highs + lower lows (LH/LL)** = downtrend. Bias: short
- **Range** = horizontal HH/LL. Trade the boundaries or wait for breakout

**Multi-timeframe confirmation**: Always check 1 timeframe up from your trade timeframe.
- Scalp (1–5 min chart) → check 15–30 min for context
- Day trade (15–30 min) → check 1H or 4H
- Swing (daily) → check weekly

## Key Indicators

### Moving Averages
**EMA (Exponential Moving Average)** — more weight on recent prices, faster to react
**SMA (Simple Moving Average)** — smoother, better for long-term structure

Key levels watched by institutions:
- **8 EMA / 21 EMA**: short-term momentum (intraday to swing)
- **50 SMA**: intermediate trend. Breakout above = bullish regime change
- **200 SMA / 200 EMA**: long-term trend. Most watched by institutional traders

**EMA Ribbon**: Plot 8, 21, 34, 55, 89 EMAs. Fanned up + price above all = strong uptrend. Ribbons compressing = momentum slowing.

**Golden Cross / Death Cross**: 50 SMA crosses above/below 200 SMA. Lagging signal but major institutional trigger.

---

### RSI (Relative Strength Index) — 14 period default
- Measures speed and change of price movements; oscillates 0–100
- > 70: overbought (in a downtrend, strong sell signal; in uptrend, can stay overbought)
- < 30: oversold (in uptrend, strong buy signal)
- **RSI divergence**: Price makes new high, RSI makes lower high → bearish divergence (warning)
- **RSI divergence**: Price makes new low, RSI makes higher low → bullish divergence (warning)
- In strong trends, RSI can stay "overbought/oversold" for extended periods

---

### MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
- Default: 12, 26, 9
- **Components**: MACD line (12 EMA − 26 EMA), Signal line (9 EMA of MACD), Histogram
- **Bullish cross**: MACD line crosses above signal line → momentum turning up
- **Bearish cross**: MACD crosses below signal line → momentum turning down
- **Zero line cross**: MACD crossing above/below zero = trend change confirmation
- **MACD divergence**: Same as RSI divergence — watch for divergence at key levels

---

### VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)
- **Intraday only** — resets at market open each day
- The single most important intraday level. Institutions benchmark to VWAP.
- Price above VWAP: bullish intraday bias. Below VWAP: bearish.
- **VWAP reclaim**: price dips below VWAP then reclaims → strong long signal
- **VWAP rejection**: price approaches VWAP from below, can't break → short signal
- In range days, VWAP acts as a magnet. In trend days, price holds one side of VWAP.

**Anchored VWAP (AVWAP)**: Anchor VWAP to a significant date (earnings, breakout, major low). Key support/resistance.

---

### Bollinger Bands (20, 2)
- Upper/lower bands = 2 standard deviations from 20-period SMA
- **Squeeze**: bands narrow → volatility contraction → expect an expansion move
- **Walk the band**: price stays on upper/lower band → strong trend, don't fade it
- **Mean reversion**: price touches upper band after a squeeze + reversal candle → fade to middle
- Band width indicator: narrow = compression, about to move

---

### Volume
Volume confirms or denies price moves:
- **Breakout + high volume**: confirmed, likely to follow through
- **Breakout + low volume**: suspect, likely to fail (trap)
- **Drop + high volume**: distribution (smart money selling)
- **Drop + low volume**: normal pullback, likely to resume uptrend
- **Climax volume**: extreme volume bar at a trend extreme → often reversal signal

## Chart Patterns

### Continuation Patterns (trade with the trend)

**Bull Flag / Bear Flag**
- Strong directional move (the flagpole) → consolidation in a tight channel (the flag)
- Entry: breakout of the channel in direction of the original trend
- Stop: below the channel low (bull flag) or above channel high (bear flag)
- Target: flagpole length projected from breakout point
- Best when: flag forms on declining volume, breakout on expanding volume

**Ascending / Descending Triangle**
- Flat top + rising lows (ascending) → bullish. Flat bottom + falling highs (descending) → bearish
- Entry: breakout of the flat side
- Target: triangle height projected from breakout

**Cup and Handle**
- Rounded bottom (cup) → brief consolidation (handle) → breakout
- Typically a longer-duration pattern (weeks to months), bullish
- Entry: handle breakout on volume

---

### Reversal Patterns

**Head and Shoulders (H&S)**
- Three peaks: left shoulder, head (highest), right shoulder → neckline break = bearish
- Target: distance from head to neckline projected down from neckline break
- Most reliable reversal pattern in technical analysis

**Double Top / Double Bottom**
- Two tests of a high (top) or low (bottom) with a swing in between
- Confirmation: break of the swing low (top) or swing high (bottom)
- Volume should be higher on first peak/trough than second

**Hammer / Shooting Star (Candlestick)**
- Hammer: small body at top, long lower wick → buyers stepped in, bullish reversal signal at support
- Shooting star: small body at bottom, long upper wick → sellers stepped in, bearish at resistance
- Must occur at a significant level (support/resistance) to be meaningful

**Engulfing Candles**
- Bullish engulfing: bearish candle followed by a larger bullish candle that engulfs it
- Bearish engulfing: bullish candle followed by a larger bearish candle
- More significant with volume confirmation at key levels

## Support & Resistance

**How to identify key levels:**
1. Prior swing highs and lows (most important)
2. Round numbers ($100, $50, $200, etc.) — psychological levels, heavy options activity
3. Previous day high/low (PDH/PDL) — intraday levels watched by all traders
4. Pre-market high/low — often acts as intraday magnet
5. Earnings gap levels — unfilled gaps often get revisited
6. Moving averages (50, 200 SMA) — dynamic support/resistance
7. VWAP and anchored VWAP

**Principle of polarity**: a broken support level often becomes resistance, and vice versa.

---

## Sector and Market Context

Before any individual trade, check:
1. **SPY / QQQ trend**: is the broad market in an uptrend, downtrend, or range?
2. **VIX level**: VIX < 15 = complacent, low volatility regime. VIX > 25 = fear, more volatility
3. **Sector strength**: is the sector your trade is in outperforming or underperforming?
4. **Relative strength**: does the stock hold up when the market pulls back? (bullish RS = strong stock)
